What is the RDSP?

The Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP) is a long-term savings program created by the Canadian government to help people with disabilities and their families save for the future. It allows contributions to grow tax-free and offers generous government support through grants and bonds. Whether you're planning ahead or supporting a loved one, the RDSP is a powerful tool to ensure financial security and peace of mind.

Why Open an RDSP?

Opening an RDSP is one of the smartest ways to build long-term financial stability for individuals with disabilities. Not only do your savings grow tax-free, but the government also contributes through matching grants and bonds — meaning more money for your future. Whether you're saving for education, living expenses, or long-term care, the RDSP helps create peace of mind and financial independence over time.

History About the RDSP

The federal government implemented the RDSPon December 1, 2008. This was the result of many years of advocacy by Planned Lifetime Advocacy Network (PLAN), families, and supporters. PLAN worked with the provinces to accommodate the RDSP by raising asset limits and eliminating claw backs. The Honorable Jim Flaherty, Minister of Finance, championed the RDSP concept within government. Employees in Finance Canada, Human Resources and Skills Development Canada, and Justice Canada, worked long hours at breakneck speed to make the idea a reality.
