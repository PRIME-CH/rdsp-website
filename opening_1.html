Opening The RDSP

You’ve decided that you’d like to open an RDSP and you want to get started. This can be done by following each step in this section
First, just a reminder of what you need to be eligible to open an RDSP:

  • You are a resident of Canada
  • You have your valid, Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • You are 59 years old or younger
  • You are eligible for the Disability Tax Credit

How To Apply To DTC ?

The DTC application process involves a few important steps. Here's a simple breakdown to help guide you:

  1. Step 1: Download the DTC Form (T2201) and fill out Part A.
  2. Step 2: Ask a qualified health professional (like your doctor or nurse practitioner) to complete Part B.
  3. Step 3: Get the signed form back, review it, and send it to the CRA by mail or online.
  4. Step 4: Wait for a response (usually takes 6–8 weeks). If denied, you can try again or file an objection.

Apply For Social Insurance Number

A Social Insurance Number (SIN) A Social Insurance Number (SIN) is a unique 9-digit number used to identify individuals for work and access to government benefits in Canada. It’s required to open an RDSP.

    Anyone over 12 can apply, and parents or legal guardians can apply for children or adults who need help. To get a SIN, gather an original ID (like a birth certificate), complete the application form from Service Canada, and submit it in person or by mail. SINs are now issued as confirmation letters. Once received, keep your SIN safe. If applying for someone else, include your ID, theirs, and legal documents proving your authority.

Filling Your Income Tax Returns

In order for the government to know how much RDSP Grant and Bond you will receive, you need to file your income taxes every year. If you have never filed income taxes or have missed a few years, you will need to file taxes for the past two years.

  There are two ways to file income taxes: you can either file taxes on your own by getting the General Income Tax and Benefit Return for the year you're filing, filling out the forms, and mailing them in, or you can get a professional to assist you if you find the process complicated.

Choosing Your Financial Situation

Before opening an RDSP, check if your current bank offers it. If not, you'll need to choose another one. Ask about location, fees, how deposits work, investment options, and withdrawal rules. Some banks may charge to move your RDSP or limit how you manage or access the funds.

Choosing Your Holder

Who is the Holder?
The Holder manages the RDSP. If you're an adult and can handle your finances, you can be the Holder—even with help.

For children:
Parents or guardians are the Holders. They can continue or share the role when the child becomes an adult.

If you can’t manage it:
A legal representative (like a parent, spouse, or Power of Attorney) can be the Holder.

Your RDSP Starter Checklist

Use this quick checklist to make sure you're good to go :

  • Do you live in Canada?
  • Do you have your Social Insurance Number and valid photo ID?
  • YHave you been approved for the Disability Tax Credit (DTC)?
  • Have you picked your financial institution?
  • Do you know who will be the RDSP Holder?

  You don’t need your DTC certificate or tax returns—your financial institution and the government handle that. Just be ready to fill out the RDSP form and, if you want the grant and bond, those forms too. Want to skip the grant or bond? Just leave those sections blank.
